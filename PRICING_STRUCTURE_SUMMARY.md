## Per-DID Overage Pricing

### How It Works
Your base subscription includes a certain number of DIDs. If you exceed that limit, you'll be charged a per-DID rate for each additional active DID.

### Example: Professional Plan
- **Base Fee:** $299/month (includes 1,000 DIDs)
- **Your DID Count:** 1,250 DIDs
- **Overage:** 250 DIDs × $1.00 = $250
- **Total Monthly Charge:** $299 + $250 = **$549/month**

### Usage Tracking
- Real-time DID count displayed in dashboard
- Email alerts at 80%, 90%, and 100% of included DIDs
- Monthly usage reports
- Easily upgrade to a higher tier to reduce per-DID costs

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What counts as an "active DID"?
**A:** Any phone number in your account with `isActive: true` status. Deactivated or archived DIDs don't count toward your limit.

### Q: Can I change plans mid-month?
**A:** Yes! Upgrades take effect immediately. Downgrades take effect at the next billing cycle. We prorate charges fairly.

### Q: What happens if I exceed my DID limit?
**A:** You'll be charged the per-DID overage rate for each additional DID. You can upgrade to a higher plan anytime to reduce costs.

### Q: Do you offer discounts for annual billing?
**A:** Yes! Annual plans save you 2 months (pay for 10, get 12). That's 16.67% off the monthly price.

### Q: Can I use my own DID providers?
**A:** Absolutely! All plans support BYO DIDs. You can import DIDs from any provider (Telnyx, Twilio, Bandwidth, etc.)

### Q: What's the difference between Basic and Professional rotation?
**A:** Basic uses rule-based rotation (round-robin, least-used). Professional uses AI that learns from your call history and optimizes in real-time based on performance data.

### Q: Is my payment information secure?
**A:** Yes! We use PayPal's vaulting system, which is PCI DSS Level 1 compliant. We never store or see your full card number.

### Q: Can I cancel anytime?
**A:** Yes, you can cancel your subscription at any time. You'll retain access until the end of your billing period.

### Q: Do unused DIDs roll over to the next month?
**A:** Your DID limit is a capacity limit, not a usage limit. If your plan includes 250 DIDs, you can have up to 250 active DIDs at any time. There's no "rollover" concept—it's just a maximum capacity.
